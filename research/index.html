Summary

The goal of my research is to better understand and empower entrepreneurs and organizations through data. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, strategy, and entrepreneurship, with a focus on how emerging technologies shape decision-making in uncertain and data-scarce environments. I study how founders, investors, and strategic decision-makers use signals—textual, visual, and behavioral—to navigate early-stage financing and innovation processes. My research applies computational methods that leverage large-scale behavioral data to extract actionable insights about innovation combining techniques from data science, natural language processing, and causal inference. My research with colleagues is cited in official reports by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) and the Publications Office of the European Union, particularly within the Joint Research Centre of the European Commission.

Methods

My methodological approach centers on applied machine learning, specializing in natural language processing (NLP) and multimodal machine learning for integrating heterogeneous data sources (text, speech, video). I have recently incorporated causal inference methods, including instrumental variables and causal discovery, to address endogeneity in observational data, and experimented with multi-agentic frameworks (simulacra). My PhD work helped me to develop competencies in network analysis, graph theory applications, and economic complexity metrics.

Highlights

  • To the best of my knowledge, we had the first multimodal machine learning classifier for venture outcomes showcased in this publication.
